How to Create a Meaningful Churn Analysis

Learn customer churn approaches to better understand how to use and interpret current data.

Steps to Create a Meaningful Churn Analysis

Analyzing churn within a company is a powerful way to analyze what is going well with its services or products and it helps identify weak areas to improve on. To better understand what “churn” is, this is described as a loss in customers or subscribers who stop using a business’s services.

As important as customer churn is, there are various ways one can create a meaningful churn analysis. These steps can be guidelines to help gain the most impactful insights.

1. Define Churn

The first important step is defining churn. By clearly defining how a business determines customer churn, this makes the “target” group easier to identify. For example, using a fictional dataset of a telco company, provided by IBM, they defined customer churn as customers who discontinued service within the past month.

2. Collect the Right Data

This step may include customer transaction history, demographics, customer support interactions, or any other relevant information that could potentially lead to customer churn.

3. Prepare the Data

Determine the best way to handle missing values, outliers, data inconsistencies, or duplicates.

4. Exploratory Analysis

Use visuals and statistics to uncover patterns, correlations, and early insights.

5. Choose a Predictive Model

Machine learning algorithms such as XGBoost can help predict customer churn. Train the model using historical data and evaluate it with validation techniques. (XGBoost was used in this project and will be covered in another blog post.)

6. Identify Important Features

Use feature importance to identify which variables strongly correlate with customer churn. This helps understand what drives customers to leave.

7. Analyze Feedback

Review survey responses and feedback to pinpoint common issues behind churn.

8. Suggest Retention Strategies

After analysis, recommend actions like personalized marketing, loyalty programs, better support, or product improvements.

These steps offer a strong foundation for customer churn analysis. But remember—data and customer behavior constantly change. Churn analysis is a continuous, evolving process to support better decision-making and retention efforts.
